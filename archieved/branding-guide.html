The vibe

A well-set broadsheet, not a SaaS dashboard.

Cream paper, near-black ink, one terracotta accent. Hard edges and blocky shadows that read as printed and deliberate. Confident and human, never slick or corporate.

Honest, not hypey

Plain language an owner actually uses. No agency jargon, no buzzwords, no inflated claims.

Confident, not loud

One accent color, used sparingly, carries all the energy. Restraint reads as competence.

Crafted, not templated

Sharp 2px borders and hard offset shadows. It looks made, not generated.

Human, not corporate

Warm cream over cold white. A real person named Thao, not a faceless studio.

Rules

Sentence case for headings, never Title Case. Uppercase only for small tracked labels. Headings set tight (line-height:1.02). Use italic Fraunces for a single emphasized phrase, not whole sentences.

03
Shape & texture

Hard edges. Blocky shadows. Paper grain.

Structure stays sharp; people stay round. Borders are 2px ink, shadows are solid offset blocks (never blur), and a faint grain overlay makes the cream feel like paper.

The contrast rule

Rounded corners appear only where they're human: the phone mockup, chat bubbles, status dots, pills. Everything structural (sections, cards, buttons) stays at 90°. That tension is intentional, not an accident.

04
What to avoid

The fastest ways to break the brand.

  • ✕ Purple/blue gradients, glassmorphism, or frosted blur. The generic SaaS look this brand rejects.
  • ✕ Pure white (#FFF) backgrounds. Always warm cream; white reads cold.
  • ✕ Soft drop shadows or glows. Shadows are solid offset blocks, full stop.
  • ✕ Rounded corners on structural elements. Rounding is for human/device elements only.
  • ✕ A second accent color. Terracotta is the only one; more dilutes it.
  • ✕ Large fields of red. The accent stays under ~5% of any view.
  • ✕ Generic fonts (Inter, Roboto, Arial). Fraunces + Hanken only.
  • ✕ Corporate jargon: "leverage," "seamless," "robust," "innovative."

● Do

  • Warm cream backgrounds
  • One terracotta accent, used rarely
  • Sharp borders + hard offset shadows
  • Sentence-case headings
  • Plain, specific owner-language
  • Alternate light/dark bands for rhythm

✕ Don't

  • Cold white or gray backgrounds
  • Multiple accents or gradients
  • Soft blurry shadows or glows
  • Title Case or ALL-CAPS headlines
  • Buzzwords and vague superlatives
  • Flat same-color sections that blend
